This is a compilation of legal terms and their definitions.

ACQUITTAL - A verdict that a criminal defendant is not guilty of the crime they are accused.

ADMISSIBLE - Used to describe evidence that may be considered in a criminal case.

AFFIDAVIT - A written statement made under oath.

APPEAL - A request of the losing party in a case to review the case and its verdict.

APPELLANT - The party appealing a court decision

APPELLATE - A court with the power to review the judgment of trial courts.

APPELLEE - The party opposing an appellant's decision.

ARRAIGNMENT - A proceeding where a criminal defendant is charged and asked to plead guilty or not guilty.

BAIL - The release of a person from prison prior to trial.

BANKRUPTCY - A legal procedure where indebted parties seek relief from a portion or all of their debts.

BENCH TRIAL - A trial without a jury.

BRIEF - A written statement during a trial explaining the arguments of one side.

BURDEN OF PROOF - The duty to prove disputed facts, vested in the plaintiff and prosecution in civil and criminal cases respectively.

CASE FILE - A collection of every document involved in a court case.

CASE LAW - Law estalbished by previous court decisions.

CLERK OF COURT - A court officer managing administrative functions of the court.

COLLATERAL - Property promised for the satisfaction of debt.

COMMUNITY SERVICE - Unpaid service given by a court as punishment for a crime.

COMPLAINT - A written statement beginning a civil lawsuit.

CONTRACT - A legal agreement between two or more parties.

CONVICTION - When the defendant in a criminal case is found guilty of the crime which they are accused of.

COURT - A government body tasked with the purpose of solving disputes.

CREDITOR - The party to whom a debtor owes money.

DAMAGES - Money the defendant pays the plaintiff if the plaintiff wins a lawsuit.

DE FACTO - Something that's unofficial or not by law.

DE JURE - Something that is official by law.

DEBTOR - Person who owes debt to a creditor.

DEFENDANT - A person being sued, or charged with a crime.

DUE PROCESS - In criminal law, the constitutional guarantee of a fair trial for the defendant.

EVIDENCE - Information presented in testimony to support the argument of one party in a court case.

FELONY - A serious crime.

FILE - To place a paper in the custody of a court.

HABEAS CORPUS - A judicial order forcing law enforcement authorities to produce a prisoner they are holding and to justify the prisoner's confinement.

HEARSAY - Evidence presented that a witness did not see or hear firsthand.

IMPEACHMENT - In a trial, the process of calling a witness's testimony to doubt.

INDICTMENT - A formal charge issues by a grand jury stating there is enough evidence of crime being committed to try a defendant.

INTERROGATORIES - A form of discovery consisting of written questions to be asked under oath.

ISSUE - The disputed point in a lawsuit.

JUDGE - An official with the authority to regulate court proceedings in a trial.

JURISDICTION - In a legal context, the legal authority of a court to decide a case.

JURY - A group of persons selected to hear evidence in a trial and decide on a verdict.

LAWSUIT - A legal action by a plaintiff against a defendant over a legal dispute.

MISDEMEANOR - An offense punishable by one year or less in prison; a lesser crime.

MISTRIAL - An invalid trial due to a fundamental error.

MOTION - A request for a decision relating to an issue in court.

OBJECTION - A formal protest raised in trial in response to an erroneous procedure or question.

PAROLE - The release of an inmate after a portion of their sentence is completed.

PETITION - The document, in a lawsuit, setting forth basic information about the debtor.

PLAINTIFF - A party that files a formal complaint and initiates a lawsuit.

PLEA - A defendant's statement of whether they plead guitly or not guilty.

PROBATION - Instead of imprisoning a guilty defendant, the convict is instead released into the community under the supervision of law enforcement.

PROCEDURE - The general manner in which civil and criminal cases are conducted.

RECORD - A written account of proceedings in a court case.

SENTENCE - The punishment given to a defendant after they have been convicted of a crime.

STATUTE - A law passed by a legislature.

SUBPOENA - An order to a witness to appear at court and give a testimony.

TESTIMONY - Evidence presented by witnesses during a court case.

VERDICT - The decision of a jury, or in case of a bench trial, the judge, on the outcome of a trial.

WARRANT - A court authorization for searching or arresting a suspect.

WITNESS - A person called up during a trial to present testimonies to support a side.

WRIT - An order directing a person on how to act, to take or not to take a certain action.
