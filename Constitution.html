This page includes a summarization of the Michigan constitution. Keep in mind that this is a simplification of the document, and a full version can be found here.


THE PREAMBLE

We, the people of the State of Michigan, grateful to Almighty God for the blessings of freedom, and earnestly desiring to secure these blessings undiminished to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish this constitution.


ARTICLE I - DECLARATION OF RIGHTS

Political power in Michigan belongs to the people, and the government is established for the purpose of serving the people.

Nobody shall be denied rights and protections on the basis of race, religion, or nationality.

All citizens are guaranteed the right to assembly and petition, freedom of religion (and no government money will go to religious institutions), freedom of speech and press, and the right to bear arms.

The military is always subordinate to civil law, and no soldier will be housed in a home against the consent of a homeowner in time of peace.

Slavery and involuntary servitude are not tolerated in Michigan, except in punishment for a crime.

No bill or law will impair the obligation of contracts.

No person or their property shall be unreasonably searched, and a warrant for searching shall not be obtained without a description of the person or item, a probable cause, and an oath.

Habeas corpus shall remain in place unless it threatens public safety.

A suitor can prosecute or defend his lawsuit in any court in the state.

Michigan has the right to trial by jury, and Michigan citizens cannot be charged twice for the same crime.

All accused shall be offered bail unless they are charged with a violent felony and committed two violent felonies within the past 15 years, charged with treason, murder, sex crimes, armed robbery, or kidnapping, or charged for a violent felony committed while on bail.

There shall be no excessive fines or bail or cruel and unusual punishment, and nobody should be forced to testify against themselves.

In libel cases, if the matter charged as libel is true, the individual accused of libel should be acquitted.

In all criminal cases the accused should have a speedy and public trial with an impartial jury, be informed of the charge and the opposing witnesses, and have the right to obtain people to support their case. They should also be granted the right to appeal and the assistance necessary for such an appeal.

Nobody should be imprisoned for debt except in cases of fraud.

Fighting against the state or supporting its enemies is treason, and one cannot be convicted of treason unless two witnesses report the same instance of treason.

The mention of certain rights does not deny other rights not mentioned.

Victims of crime shall have their dignity and privacy respected, and have the right to be updated about and participate in court proceedings and receive restitution.

In Michigan, marriage is only recognized as the union between a man and a woman.

Public universities and school districts shall not discriminate against or grant preferential treatment to students based on race, sex, national origin, etc, nor shall the state.

Stem cell research legal under federal law is legal in Michigan; human cloning is still prohibited in the state. Stem cells can only be taken from an embryo within fourteend days of division, and must be taken with informed consent. Nobody is permitted to purchase stem cells for research and research must be conducted in accordance to state and federal law.


ARTICLE II - ELECTIONS

Citizens who are eighteen can vote as long as they have lived six months in the state and meet their local residency requirements.

People may be excluded from voting on account of mental incompetence or incarceration.

For presidential elections, the legislature can lower residence requirements. Those who meet the voting requirements for a different state are not permitted to vote in Michigan.

All eligible voters have the right to a secret ballot, and those overseas shall have an absentee ballot mailed to them should they apply. Ballots are offered in which citizens can vote for all candidates of a party. Those who conduct business with the secretary of state are automatically registered to vote unless they decline; one can also register to vote by submitting an application in person within fourteen days of the election or mailing in an application before that. In the forty days before an election a registered voter can obtain an absent ballot without giving a reason. Elections will be audited to ensure integrity.

Election day is the first Tuesday after the first Monday of November on even numbered years.

A board of four canvassers shall be chosen with no candidates or a majority from one party.

If 25% of voters who voted in the prior election of an elected politician's petition to remove the politician, a recall vote may be called.

The people have the power to invoke initiative or referendum, or the right to propose and reject new laws or approve or reject enacted laws respectively.

No member of the House of Representatives shall serve more than three terms in twelve years, nor a Senator more than two in twenty four years.

ARTICLE III - GENERAL GOVERNMENT

The seat of government is in Lansing, and shall be split into the executive, judicial, and legislative branch; there shall be a Great Seal of Michigan.

The militia shall be organized, equipped and disciplined as provided by law.

This state may enter into agreements with other states, the US, or Canada.

The state shall only be involved in internal improvements in a manner prescribed by law.

Common law not contradicting the constitution shall remain in place until they are repealed.

Legislature or the governor may request constitutionality of a law from the Supreme Court.

ARTICLE IV - LEGISLATIVE BRANCH

The Senate and House hold legislative power, with the Senate consisting of 38 members serving four year terms and the House consisting of 110 members servering two year terms.

There will be an independent citizens redistricting committee consisting of 13 qualified commissioners, chosen by the secretary of state. The commissioners need to draft up plans for redistricting in an impartial manner and vote on a plan to adopt.

Senators and representatives must be citizens, 21, and not convicted of felony within the past 20 years.

No government employee or officer may be a senator or representative.

No legislator, during their term, shall receive civil appointment from the state, nor shall they be interested in contracts causing a significant conflict of interest.

Legislators are exempt from civil arrest during sessions of legislature.

A state officers compensation commission is to be established, consisting of seven qualified members who are tasked with determining the salary of government officers.

Legislature shall meet on the second Wednesday of January of each year.

A majority of legislators present constitutes a quorum, and there shall be a bi-partisan legislative council of legislators appointed in a legal manner.

Each house will pick officers to manage proceedings, and with agreement of two thirds of one house may expel a member from a session.

Legislators can create committees; on all bills and resolutions, votes are to be recorded for the public to see.

Each house shall keep a journal of its proceedings, and all opens shall be open unless public security is at risk.

Neither house shall adjourn for more than two intervening calendar days without the other's consent.

Bills can originate in either house, with laws styled with "The People of the State of Michigan enact".

Laws shall only contain one purpose, expressed in the title, and the purpose must not be changed upon passage.

Laws can only be passed after being in a house's possession for five days, and read three times before passing.

Laws shall only take effect after 90 days of passing unless given immediate effect by a two-thirds majority.

At special sessions only bills on subjects stated in the governor's proclamation may be passed.

Local or special acts can only be passed where general legislation is not applicable, and must be approved by two thirds of legislators from the affected district.

Two thirds of legislators must agree to appropriate public funds for local or private uses.

A general appropriation bill must pass each fiscal year before any other appropriation bill may be passed.

Any law instating a tax must state the tax.

Bills passed by the legislature are presented to the governor. The governor may approve the bill and make it law, or veto it, with a two thirds majority vote in the legislature required to pass it.

Bills, other than appropriation bills, can provide that it will not become law unless approved by a majority of voters.

All laws enacted are to be published in book form within 60 days.

No general revision of laws shall be made.

The legislature can empower a joint committee to suspend any rule or regulation of the administrative agency continuing to the end of the end of the next regular legislative session.

The legislature may decide what constitutes a vacant position and how to fill it.

To ensure continuity of the government in the case of an enemy attack, the legislature is permitted to temporarily replace those unavailable for the duties of their office.

The drinking age is 21 for beverages containing more than half a percent of alcohol.

The legislature can authorize lotteries in a manner provided by law.

The legislature may provide for incorporation of ports and authorize them to work on internal improvements.

No general law for the incorporation of companies for banking shall be enacted, amended, or repealed unless by two thirds of legislators.

Civil cases may have less than 12 jurors.

The legislature can provide indeterminate sentences as punishment for crime.

There shall be no death penalty in Michigan.

The legislature can authorize the employment of chaplains in correctional facilities.

The legislature may enact laws providing for resolutions of disputes by public employees and enact laws relative to hours and condition of employment.

The legislature may regulate atomic energy for the safety of Michigan citizens.

Public health and welfare is of primary public concern, as is conservation of natural resources.

The legislature may elect a certified public accountant as auditor general for an eight year term. They shall post audits of the financial transactions of al branches and departments of the state. The auditor general shall report to the government annually and be exempt from classified civil service.

One can only serve as state representative three times, and as state senator twice, with a period of greater than half a term to be counted as a full term.

ARTICLE V - EXECUTIVE BRANCH

Executive power is vested in the governor.

Executive agencies are to be allocated within not more than 20 departments. The governor may amend executive organization, except in the independent citizen's redistricting commission. The head of each department shall be a single executive, appointed by the governor.

Temporary commissions of less than two years may be established.

The majority of an examination board for a profession should be made up of members of that profession.

Appointments not disapproved by the senate within 60 days stand confirmed.

In the case of vacancies of positions where senate approval is necessary, new appointees must be approved by the senate.

Each department is under the supervision of the governor, who may initiate court proceeddings to enforce compliance of the law in public officers.

Single executives of a department shall keep their offices at the seat of government.

The governor maintains the power to remove or suspend any official for neglect of duty or corruption, and appoint a temporary replacement in case of suspension.

The governor is the commander-in-chief of the armed forces.

Should there be a vacancy in the legislature the governor can issue writs of election to fill it.

The governor has the power to grant pardons.

The governor may convene the legislature on extraordinary occasions, and away from the seat of government.

The governor shall communicate to the legislature at sessions measures they deem desirable.

At a time fixed by law, the governor shall submit a budget for the next fiscal period and a general appropriations bill, and the governor may disapprove of distinct items appropriating money in the bill.

Should revenue fall below the revenue estimates for a fiscal period reductions in expenditure shall be made.

The governor, lieutenant governor, secretary of state, and attorney general are to serve four year terms, with the latter three nominated by party conventions. Vacancies in the latter two are to be filled by the governor.

To be governor or lieutenant governor one must be at least 30 and have been a registered elector for at least 4 years.

The executive officers are to receive compensation for services, with compensation not to change throughout their term.

The governor shall be provided a residence at the seat of government and an allowance to maintain it.

The lieutenant governor is the president of the senate, only granted voting privileges when the senate is divided.

Should the governor die or be removed, the lieutenant governor, secretary of state, and attorney general shall replace them in that order, and the successor is to be given an equal salary as the previous governor.

There shall be a state transportation commission of six members, appointed for three year terms, the director being the principal executive officer of the transportation department.

There shall be a civil rights commission of eight members, appointed for four year terms, to investigate alleged discrimination and ensure equal rights.

No executive is to be elected for more than two terms.

ARTICLE VI - JUDICIAL BRANCH

The state's judicial power is split into the supreme court, court of appeals, trial court, probate court, and multiple courts of limited jurisdiction.

The supreme court shall consist of seven justices serving eight year terms elected in a non-partisan election, with one chief justice.

The supreme court has general control over courts, but cannot remove judges.

The supreme court shall establish and modify the procedure in all courts.

Decisions of the supreme court shall be in writing.

The supreme court shall have control of its staff, and the expenditure of money it appropriated.

The court of appeals shall consist of nine justices serving six year terms elected in a non-partisan manner, to oversee divisions drawn on county lines, with their procedure provided by the supreme court.

The state shall be divided into various circuits along county lines, with elected circuit judges serving six year terms. The court shall meet quarterly and have jurisdiction in all matters not prohibited by law.

The clerk of judicial purposes in a county, or a similar officer, shall be the clerk of that county's circuit court.

There shall be a probate court in each county, with jurisdiction and power to be provided by law. The probate court shall have original jurisdiction in cases involving juvenile delinquents.

One or more judges of probate serving six year terms will be elected in a non-partisan manner.

No judge shall be paid from the fees of their office.

The salaries of all judges shall be uniform and may be increased but not increased throughout a term.

All courts shall be courts of record and have a common seal. Justices must be licensed to practice law in the state and have been licensed for five years. Nobody over 70 may be a judge.

When a judge removes their domicile beyond the territory where they were elected, they shall have vacated their office.

A judge can't be appointed or elected to another office except during their term and for a year after it.

A judge from the court of appeals or circuit or probate courts can be an incumbent candidate by filing an affidavit of candidacy.

A vacancy shall occur if a judge dies, is removed, resigns, or vacates the office, and vacancies shall be filled through appointment by the governor.

For reasonable cause not sufficient for impeachment a judge can be removed at the agreement of two thirds of legislators.

The courts and judges shall not exercise the power of appointment to public office.

All final decisions or rulings by any administrative agency shall be subject to direct review by the courts.

Justices and judges shall be conservators of the peace in their respective jurisdiction.

A judicial tenure commission of nine members serving three year terms to recommend the supreme court to censure, suspend, or remove a judge when necessary.

ARTICLE VII - LOCAL GOVERNMENT

Each organized county shall be a body corporate with powers and immunities provided by law.

Any county can frame, adopt, amend, or repeal county charters, by a two-thirds majority vote from electors.

Reorganized counties must have at least 16 townships unless approved by voters in affected counties.

There shall be a sheriff, clerk, treasurer, register of deeds, and prosecuting attorney elected in each county for four year terms, with the former three holding their offices at the county seat.

The sheriff may be required to renew their security and in doing so their office will be vacant. The county is not responsible for their actions, and they shall hold no other position than civil defense.

A board of supervisors shall be made from one member from each township with powers provided by law. They have the power to fix compensation for county officers not otherwise provided by law.

A county seat shall not be removed unless its replacement is approved by two thirds of the board of supervisors and a majority of electors in that county.

No county shall incure debt above ten percent its assessed valuation.

A navigable stream shall not be bridged or damned without permission from the board.

Two or more neighboring counties can merge if a majority of electors in affected counties consent, and the board of supervisors in a county can merge townships.

When authorized by the board any county can intervene in actions involving privately owned public utility services.

Counties may take control of highways within their limits, with county road commissioners.

Townships are bodies with powers and immunities; in each township there shall be a supervisor, clerk, treasurer, and trustees elected for two to four year terms.

Townships and cities shall not grant any public utility franchise not subject to revocation at the will of the township unless approved by a majority of voters in a township.

A township may be dissolved if it is entirely included in the boundaries of one village.

The legislature shall provide laws that limit ad valorem tax rate in cities as well as the power to borrow money.

The electors in cities may adopt and amend its charter. The enumeration of power does not restrict other powers.

Any city can acquire and maintain parks, boulevards, cementeries, and hospitals, as well as public service facilities for supplying basic utilities.

No city shall loan its credit for any private purposes, or public purposes not provided by law.

The legislature may establish additional governments in metropolitan areas.

The legislature shall authorize multiple towns or counties to enter into contractual undertakings, share responsbilities of services with one another, and transfer responsibilities or lend their credit to one another.

No entity may use a highway, street, or other public space in a county or town without the authority of the county or town.

No franchise or license shall be granted by any township, city, or village for more than 30 years.

The legislature shall not vacate or alter any road or public place under the jurisdiction of a county, township, or other subdivision.

Any political subdivision empowered to prepare budgets shall adopt budgets only after a public hearing.

Any elected officer of a political subdivison can be removed in a manner provided by law.

Powers granted to political subdivisions in this constitution shall be construed in their favor.

ARTICLE VIII - EDUCATION

Education is forever encouraged in Michigan, and there shall be a free public and non-discriminatory elementary and secondary school system. No public money is to directly or indirectly maintain any private school.

Leadership over public education, except higher education, is vested in a state board of education. There shall be a superintendent, headed with execution of the board's policies. The board shall consist of eight members serving eight year terms nominated by party conventions.

The legislature shall appropriate money to maintain public universities. The board of each public university shall have general supervision over its institution and its funds.

The legislature shall provide financial support of community colleges which shall be supervised by locally elected boards. There shall also be state boards consisting of eight members serving eight year terms to advise the state board of education on matters regarding such colleges.

Services for disabled persons will always be supported.

The legislature shall establish public libraries.

ARTICLE IX - FINANCE AND TAXATION

The legislature shall impose taxes to support the government; this power is to never be surrendered.

The legislature shall provide a uniform ad valorem tax of tangible property and provide for the determination of true cash value of such property.

Non-profit religious and educational organizations are exempt from property taxes.

The government shall assess the state of property of public service businesses and other designated property for the collection of taxes thereon.

No income tax graduated as to rate or base shall be imposed in the state, and sales tax is 6% for sales excluding the sale of prescription drugs or unprepared food.

There shall be a state school aid fund used exclusively to aid school districts, higher education, and school employees.

No evidence of state indebtedness shall be issued except for debts authorized pursuant to this constitution.

Public bodies shall have power to borrow money and issue securities evidencing debt.

The legislature may authorize the state to issue its full faith and credit notes and pledge undedicated revenues to be received within the same fiscal year. The state may borrow money in the long term, provided by acts adopted by a vote of two-thirds of legislators and a majority of electors.

The state may borrow for the purpose of making loans to school districts. The school district shall levy for debt service until the loan is repaid.

No money shall be paid out of state treasury except for appropriations made by law, and the credit of the state shall not be granted to any person or corporation.

The state shall not subscribe to the stock of any company unless the money invested accumulated for government pensions or are endowment funds created for charitable causes.

No state money shall be deposited into banks other than those organized by law. The legislature shall provide for annual accounting of all public moneys and audit counties by competent state authority.

Procedures for examination and adjustment of claims against the state shall be provided by law. All financial records and audit reports of the state shall be public.

Each pension plan in the state shall be a contractual obligation, and financial benefits for services in a fiscal year shall be funded during said fiscal year.

Property taxes and other local taxes can't be increased above certain limitations without voter approval, and there shall also be a limit on the total amount of taxes which may be imposed by the legislature in any fiscal year, not to be changed without approval from a majority of qualified electors.

The revenue limit may only be exceeded if the governor requests the legislature to declare an emergency and specifies the specific plan for resolving the emergency, and the legislature approves through a two-thirds majority.

No government expense shall be incurred which exceed the revenue limit. The state is prohibited from reducing the state financed proportion of the necessary cost of any existing service required of units of local government.

The proportion of total state spending to all units of local government shall not be reduced below the proportion in effect in the fiscal year of 1978-79.

Units of local government are prohibited from levying tax not authorized by law unless approved by a majority of voters.

Any taxpayer shall have standing to bring suit to the Michigan State Court of Appeals.

The Michigan natural resources trust fund is established to manage state owned lands. It can acquire land or rights based on environmental importance or beauty, and develop it. The legislature shall also establish a trust fund board to recommend projects to be funded.

The Michigan state parks endowment fund is established for improvements and maintenance of Michigan State Parks, as well as acquisition of land. The money in the fund shall be spent in a manner provided by law.

Six percent of proceeds from tobacco are to be used to further quality of health care.

The Michigan veteran's trust fund is established, with a board of trustees consisting of appointed honorably discharged veterans who will administer the fund.

The Michigan conservation and recreation legacy fund is established. It is split into the forest recreation account, the game and fish protection account, the offroad vehicle account, the recreation improvement account, the snowmobile account, the state park improvement account, and the waterways account, each tasked with improving a certain area of Michigan's environment and recreation.

The Michigan game and fish protection trust fund and Michigan nongame fish and wildlife trust fund are established.

ARTICLE X - PROPERTY

The disabilities of coverture of property are abolished. The property a woman had prior to marriage shall remain hers. Dower may be relinquished by law.

Private property shall not be taken for public use without compensation; homestead in the amount between 750 and 3500 shall be exempt from forced sale on execution or other process of any court.

Procedures relating to escheats shall be provided by law.

The legislature shall have jurisdiction over all state owned lands, and by a two thirds vote can adopt any part of such lands in the state land reserve.

Aliens who are residents of the state shall enjoy equal rights and privileges in property as citizens.

ARTICLE XI - AMENDMENTS

All officials shall swear the following oath before entering office: I do solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will support the Constitution of the United States and the constitution of this state, and that I will faithfully discharge the duties of the office of .......... according to the best of my ability.

The terms of state officials will begin at noon on the first of January after their election.

The state and its subdivisions shall not grant any extra compensation to any public officer after the service has been rendered.

Custodians of public moneys shall not be legislators or other office until he has made an accounting of all sums for which he may be liable.

The classified state civil service shall consist of all positions in the state service not filled by popular election and other specified exempt positions. The civil service commission shall be non-salaried and composed of four members appointed for eight year terms. The administration of the commission is vested in a state personnel director. The commission is tasked with classifying positions in the classified service. No person unqualified by the commission shall be appointed or promoted in the classified service.

A school district or other governing unit can establish, modify, or discontinue a merit system for employees other than teachers under contract.

The house of representatives shall have the sole power to impeach officials for misconduct. Three representatives are to be elected to prosecute the impeachment, with each impeachment tried by the senate. If two thirds of senators convict the defendant, they shall be punished accordingly.

A person is ineligible for election or appointment to public office if within 20 years they have been convicted of a felony involving breach of public trust.

Amendments may be proposed to the constitution and must be approved by two thirds of legislators. They may also be proposed by petition of electors, and approved by a majority of electors.

At the general election of 1978 and every 16th year afterwards the question of general revision shall be submitted to electors. If approved, each district shall send a delegate to a convention. The convention, by two thirds vote, will send proposed constitutions or amendments to voters, to be passed if voted on by a majority of electors.

If any part of an article or section of the constitution is held unconstitutional, the remaining parts of that article or section shall remain.
